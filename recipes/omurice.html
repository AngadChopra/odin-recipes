omurice, a dish that seems so simple yet can eb considered the pinnacle of an omelette, it is technique personified , the mastery displayed while making it is impressive. Its difficult is that it similar to a french omelette where there is no browning, yet is still slightly gooey in the middle to get that nice shape. Yet despite all this difficulty it is just simply an omelette with some rice

ingredients

  • Large eggs
  • Cooked Japanese Rice
  • chicken thigh
  • onion
  • green peas
  • ketchup
  • worcesters sauce
  • butter

steps

    THE RICE
  1. Preferably start with frozen leftover rice
  2. dice onions and portion the chicken out
  3. add a tbsp of butter and sweat onions out
  4. then add chicken when onions start to brown
  5. cook chicken until no longer pink
  6. add ketchup and 5 tbsp of worcester sauce
  7. add the leftover rice in small clumps, then salt and add your peas
    8. THE EGGS
  8. crack two eggs cutting the eggs instead of whisking
  9. add salt
  10. add butter to pan the add eggs
  11. stir eggs in a spiral motion with chopsticks
  12. stop when eggs are half cooked
  13. the use the ton ton ton method to create a spiral
  14. flip on top of your plated rice
  15. Viola
